Cirba Solutions South Carolina Facility

Columbia, SC, USA

Planned

Facility Overview

The Cirba Solutions South Carolina Facility in Columbia represents a significant expansion of the company's battery recycling capabilities in the southeastern United States. Currently in the planning stage, this facility will be designed to process lithium-ion batteries and manufacturing scrap at a large scale, supporting the growing battery manufacturing ecosystem in the region.

With a planned processing capacity of 60,000 tonnes per year, the Columbia facility will be one of Cirba Solutions' largest operations. The facility will employ advanced technologies to safely and efficiently recover valuable materials from batteries, including lithium, nickel, cobalt, and copper, contributing to a more sustainable and domestic battery supply chain.

This investment in South Carolina aligns with the state's growing role as a hub for electric vehicle and battery manufacturing. The facility will create approximately 300 new jobs and represents a significant step in building a circular economy for battery materials in North America.

Processing Technology

The planned South Carolina facility will utilize state-of-the-art processing technologies:

  • Advanced battery sorting and preparation systems
  • Automated disassembly of battery packs and modules
  • Efficient mechanical processing for material separation
  • Advanced hydrometallurgical systems for metal recovery
  • On-site precursor production capabilities
  • Quality control systems for battery-grade materials

Regional Significance

The South Carolina facility will be strategically positioned in the growing southeastern battery manufacturing corridor. South Carolina and neighboring states have attracted significant investments in electric vehicle manufacturing and battery production. The Columbia facility will contribute to the local economy by creating approximately 300 new jobs and will support the development of a robust domestic battery supply chain, reducing dependency on imported materials and enhancing national energy security.
