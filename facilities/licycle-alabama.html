Facility Overview

The Li-Cycle Alabama Spoke facility, located in Tuscaloosa, Alabama, began operations in 2022. This facility was strategically placed to support the growing EV manufacturing ecosystem in the Southeastern United States, which has seen significant investment from automotive and battery manufacturers.

The Alabama Spoke employs Li-Cycle's Generation 3 technology, which uses a submerged shredding process to safely and efficiently process full lithium-ion battery packs without discharging or dismantling them first. This approach improves safety by minimizing fire risk and increases processing efficiency.

With a processing capacity of 5,000 tonnes per year, the facility can handle various forms of lithium-ion battery materials, including manufacturing scrap and end-of-life batteries, contributing to the creation of a closed-loop battery supply chain in North America.

Processing Technology

Li-Cycle's Generation 3 technology at the Alabama Spoke features a submerged shredding process that offers several advantages:

  • Allows direct processing of full battery packs without disassembly
  • Significantly reduces fire risk by conducting operations underwater
  • Increases efficiency by eliminating the need for discharge or manual dismantling
  • Enables processing of batteries in various states of charge
  • Produces high-quality black mass suitable for further refining
