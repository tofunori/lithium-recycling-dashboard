Operating

Facility Overview

The Cirba Solutions Ohio Facility in Lancaster is one of the company's established battery recycling operations. This facility has recently undergone significant expansion to increase its capacity and capabilities in response to growing demand for battery recycling in North America.

The Lancaster facility processes a wide range of battery types, including lithium-ion batteries from electric vehicles, consumer electronics, and energy storage systems. The facility employs a combination of mechanical and hydrometallurgical processes to safely and efficiently recover valuable battery materials, including lithium, nickel, cobalt, and copper.

In 2023, Cirba Solutions announced the expansion of the Lancaster facility, which was supported in part by a $75 million grant from the U.S. Department of Energy through the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law. This expansion enhances the company's capacity to process lithium-ion batteries and supports the growing domestic battery supply chain.

Processing Technology

The Ohio facility employs a multi-step process for battery recycling:

  • Battery discharge and safe handling protocols
  • Mechanical processing for size reduction and component separation
  • Advanced sorting technologies to separate materials
  • Hydrometallurgical processing for metal recovery
  • Purification systems to achieve battery-grade specifications
  • Material refinement for reuse in new batteries

Regional Significance

The Ohio facility is strategically positioned in the Midwest region, which has a growing concentration of EV and battery manufacturing operations. The Lancaster facility contributes to the local economy by providing skilled jobs and supports the development of a domestic battery supply chain. The facility's expansion, supported by federal funding, highlights its importance in the national strategy for critical material recovery and sustainability.
