Facility Overview

The American Battery Technology Company (ABTC) Nevada Recycling Plant is located at the Tahoe Reno Industrial Center, one of the largest industrial parks in the United States. This facility represents ABTC's flagship battery recycling operation, utilizing the company's proprietary processes to recover critical battery materials from lithium-ion batteries.

With a processing capacity of approximately 20,000 tonnes per year, the Nevada plant is designed to handle various forms of lithium-ion battery materials, including manufacturing scrap and end-of-life batteries. The facility employs ABTC's innovative hydrometallurgical battery recycling technologies, which offer lower environmental impacts compared to traditional pyrometallurgical methods.

A key differentiator of the ABTC approach is the integration of extraction technologies with recycling processes, allowing the company to produce battery-grade materials for direct use in new battery manufacturing. This vertically integrated model supports ABTC's strategy to develop a comprehensive domestic battery materials supply chain.

Company Information

American Battery Technology Company (ABTC) is a critical battery materials company focused on recycling, extraction, and qualification of battery metals. ABTC implements its technologies through a closed-loop, environmentally sound process design that combines recycling of end-of-life batteries with primary extraction of battery metals from domestic resources.

Processing Technology

ABTC's Nevada plant employs a unique hydrometallurgical recycling process:

  • Initial mechanical pre-processing and material separation
  • Low-temperature selective leaching processes
  • Advanced separation and purification systems
  • Direct precipitation of battery materials
  • Quality assurance and materials characterization
  • Integrated manufacturing of battery-grade products

Regional Significance

The ABTC Nevada Recycling Plant is strategically located in the Tahoe Reno Industrial Center, in proximity to Tesla's Gigafactory and other technology companies. This location provides logistical advantages for sourcing manufacturing scrap and supplying recycled materials back to battery manufacturers. The facility contributes to Nevada's growing clean energy economy and supports the development of a regional battery materials ecosystem in the western United States.
