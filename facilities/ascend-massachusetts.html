Facility Overview

The Ascend Elements Massachusetts Operations in Worcester serve as the company's original R&D center and commercial recycling facility. This location is where Ascend Elements (formerly Battery Resourcers) was founded and where the company's proprietary Hydro-to-Cathode™ technology was developed and first commercialized.

The Worcester facility focuses on both research and development of advanced battery recycling technologies and commercial-scale battery recycling operations. The site processes lithium-ion batteries from consumer electronics, electric vehicles, and energy storage systems, recovering critical materials and transforming them into new battery materials.

As the company's innovation center, the Massachusetts facility houses Ascend Elements' advanced laboratories for battery materials analysis, quality control, and next-generation recycling technology development. The expertise and processes developed at this location have informed the company's expansion to other regions, including their larger facilities in Georgia and Kentucky.

Technical Specifications

  • Processing Capacity: 10,000 tonnes per year
  • Technology: Hydro-to-Cathode™ direct recycling
  • Operational Since: 2015 (expanded 2021)
  • Feedstock: Consumer electronics, EV batteries, manufacturing scrap
  • Output: Battery-grade cathode materials and precursors
  • R&D Capabilities: Advanced material synthesis and testing

Environmental Impact

  • GHG Reduction: Up to 90% lower carbon emissions than conventional processes
  • Water Management: Closed-loop water recycling systems
  • Waste Diversion: Near-zero waste to landfill operations
  • Resource Recovery: >95% recovery rate for critical materials
  • Energy Efficiency: Process optimized for lower energy consumption

Company Information

Ascend Elements is a leading provider of sustainable battery material solutions that transform the lithium-ion battery supply chain. The company's Hydro-to-Cathode™ technology directly synthesizes new cathode active material from spent lithium-ion cells, enabling more efficient recovery of critical battery metals and reducing the environmental impact of battery production.

Processing Technology

The Massachusetts facility utilizes the company's proprietary processes:

  • Battery safe handling and preparation
  • Material shredding and separation
  • Advanced hydrometallurgical processing
  • Direct cathode precursor synthesis
  • Material testing and quality validation
  • Pilot-scale process development

Research & Innovation

As the company's original innovation center, the Massachusetts facility continues to drive advancement in battery recycling technologies. The on-site R&D lab focuses on improving recovery efficiencies, developing processes for emerging battery chemistries, and supporting the commercialization of next-generation recycling techniques. The facility collaborates with nearby universities and research institutions to advance sustainable battery material technologies.
