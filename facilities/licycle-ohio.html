Li-Cycle Ohio Spoke

Warren, OH, USA

Under Construction

Facility Overview

The Li-Cycle Ohio Spoke facility is currently under construction in Warren, Ohio. This strategic location was selected to support the growing battery manufacturing ecosystem in the Midwest region, which has seen significant investment from automotive and battery manufacturers.

With a planned processing capacity of 15,000 tonnes per year, the Ohio Spoke will be one of Li-Cycle's larger facilities, designed to handle various forms of lithium-ion battery materials, including manufacturing scrap and end-of-life batteries from electric vehicles, energy storage systems, and consumer electronics.

This facility will utilize mechanical processing technology to safely process lithium-ion batteries and produce black mass, which contains critical metals such as lithium, cobalt, and nickel. This black mass will be transported to Li-Cycle's Hub facility for further processing into battery-grade materials, supporting the creation of a closed-loop battery supply chain in North America.

Company Information

Li-Cycle is a leading global lithium-ion battery resource recovery company and the largest lithium-ion battery recycler in North America. The company's innovative "Spoke & Hub" technologies allow for the recovery of critical materials from all types of lithium-ion batteries.

Processing Technology

The Ohio Spoke will utilize mechanical processing technology to safely process lithium-ion batteries:

  • Initial sorting and preparation of incoming materials
  • Safe shredding process to reduce battery materials to smaller components
  • Material separation to isolate black mass, copper, aluminum, and plastics
  • Quality control to ensure consistent output materials
  • Preparation for transport to Hub facility for final processing

Regional Significance

The Ohio Spoke is strategically positioned to serve the growing battery manufacturing ecosystem in the Midwest. The region has seen significant investment in EV and battery production facilities, making it an ideal location for battery recycling operations. The facility will create approximately 30-40 new jobs and support the development of a domestic battery supply chain.
