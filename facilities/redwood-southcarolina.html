Facility Overview

The Redwood Materials South Carolina Campus represents one of the largest investments in battery recycling and materials production in North America. Located in Berkeley County, South Carolina, this $3.5 billion facility is currently under construction and will significantly expand Redwood's capacity to recycle lithium-ion batteries and produce sustainable battery materials.

Once operational, the facility will have the capacity to produce enough anode and cathode material for more than one million electric vehicles annually. The campus will support the growing battery manufacturing ecosystem in the southeastern United States, which has seen substantial investments from automotive manufacturers and battery producers.

Like Redwood's Nevada facility, the South Carolina Campus will implement the company's integrated approach - recycling lithium-ion batteries and producing new battery materials on-site. This vertical integration creates a true circular economy for battery materials, reducing the need for newly mined resources and lowering the environmental impact of battery production.

Company Information

Redwood Materials is a circular supply chain company founded by JB Straubel, the former CTO and co-founder of Tesla. The company is focused on producing sustainable battery materials and recycling lithium-ion batteries to create a closed-loop battery supply chain in North America.

Processing Technology

The South Carolina Campus will utilize advanced integrated processes:

  • Battery disassembly and mechanical processing
  • Hydrometallurgical processing for metal extraction
  • Chemical refining to battery-grade materials
  • Cathode active material production
  • Anode copper foil manufacturing
  • Quality control and advanced automation systems

Regional Significance

The South Carolina Campus is strategically located in the rapidly growing southeastern battery manufacturing corridor. This region has attracted major investments from automotive manufacturers like BMW, Mercedes-Benz, Hyundai, and battery producers. The facility is expected to create approximately 1,500 jobs and will be a cornerstone of the domestic battery supply chain, reducing dependency on imported materials.
