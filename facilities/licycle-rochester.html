Eastman Business Park, Rochester, NY, USA

Planned

Facility Overview

The Li-Cycle North American Hub, located at Eastman Business Park in Rochester, NY, is designed as a centralized processing facility to refine black mass from Li-Cycle's network of Spoke facilities into battery-grade materials.

This Hub will be a first-of-its-kind facility in North America, capable of processing black mass into critical battery materials including lithium, nickel, and cobalt. The Hub represents a key component of Li-Cycle's strategy to create a closed-loop battery supply chain in North America.

Once operational, the Rochester Hub will be able to process material equivalent to approximately 225,000 electric vehicles per year. By processing black mass generated at Li-Cycle's Spoke facilities, the Hub will demonstrate how urban mining of critical materials from spent lithium-ion batteries can help reduce reliance on mining of virgin materials.

Technical Specifications

  • Processing Capacity: 35,000 tonnes per year
  • Technology: Hydrometallurgical processing
  • Planned Start: 2025
  • Feedstock: Black mass from Spoke facilities
  • Output: Battery-grade lithium, nickel, and cobalt
  • EV Equivalent: ~225,000 EVs per year

Environmental Impact

  • Emissions Reduction: Lower carbon footprint compared to primary material extraction
  • Resource Conservation: Reduces need for primary mining
  • Energy Efficiency: Optimized hydrometallurgical processes
  • Water Management: Closed-loop water systems
  • Circular Economy: Enables reuse of battery materials

Processing Technology

The Rochester Hub will employ an advanced hydrometallurgical process to recover battery-grade materials from black mass. This wet chemistry process involves:

  • Selective leaching of critical metals
  • Impurity removal and purification
  • Metal separation and recovery
  • Product finishing to meet battery-grade specifications

This approach allows for high recovery rates of lithium, nickel, cobalt, and other valuable materials while maintaining environmental responsibility.
