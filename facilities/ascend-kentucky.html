Facility Overview

Ascend Elements' Apex 1 facility in Hopkinsville, Kentucky represents one of the most significant investments in sustainable battery materials manufacturing in North America. Currently under construction, this landmark facility will be North America's largest sustainable battery materials manufacturing plant when completed, with an estimated $1 billion investment.

The Apex 1 facility will utilize Ascend Elements' proprietary Hydro-to-Cathode™ direct precursor synthesis process to transform recycled lithium-ion battery materials into new, sustainable cathode active materials (CAM) for use in new batteries. With a planned capacity to produce enough CAM to power 250,000 electric vehicles annually, this facility will play a crucial role in creating a closed-loop, sustainable battery materials supply chain in North America.

Strategically located in Kentucky's growing battery manufacturing corridor, the Apex 1 facility will create approximately 400 jobs and serve as a cornerstone of the domestic EV battery supply chain. The project is being supported by federal and state incentives, including grants through the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law, reflecting its importance to the U.S. clean energy transition.

Company Information

Ascend Elements is a leading provider of sustainable battery material solutions that transform the lithium-ion battery supply chain. The company's Hydro-to-Cathode™ technology directly synthesizes new cathode active material from spent lithium-ion cells, enabling more efficient recovery of critical battery metals and reducing the environmental impact of battery production.

Processing Technology

The Apex 1 facility will employ advanced manufacturing technologies:

  • Integrated hydrometallurgical processing
  • Advanced co-precipitation reactors
  • Proprietary precursor synthesis systems
  • High-temperature calcination
  • Advanced quality control and material testing
  • Automated packaging and logistics systems

Regional Significance

The Apex 1 facility is strategically located in Kentucky's emerging battery manufacturing corridor. The southeast and central U.S. regions have attracted significant investments in EV and battery manufacturing, with multiple battery gigafactories announced or under construction within a day's drive. The facility will create approximately 400 high-skilled jobs in Christian County and represents a cornerstone of the domestic battery supply chain, supporting U.S. energy independence and economic growth in the clean energy sector.
