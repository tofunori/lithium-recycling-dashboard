Li-Cycle Arizona Spoke

Gilbert, Arizona, USA

Operating

Facility Overview

Li-Cycle's Arizona Spoke is located in Gilbert, Arizona, at the Fiesta Tech Campus. It commenced operations in May 2022 and is capable of processing all kinds of lithium-ion batteries and manufacturing scrap, regardless of chemistry, form factor or condition, including full electric vehicle batteries without the need for discharging or dismantling.

This facility is the first to utilize Li-Cycle's proprietary 'Generation 3' technology, which allows for direct processing of full EV battery packs. The facility is strategically located close to Li-Cycle's existing battery and manufacturing scrap supply network in the Southwestern U.S., which optimizes logistics and other efficiencies for recycling services.

The 140,000 square-foot facility significantly expands Li-Cycle's recycling capacity in North America and supports the growing EV manufacturing ecosystem in the region.

Technical Specifications

  • Size: 140,000 square feet
  • Processing Capacity: 10,000 tonnes per year
  • Technology: Generation 3 submerged shredding
  • Start of Operations: May 2022
  • Feedstock: Manufacturing scrap and end-of-life batteries
  • Output: Black mass

Environmental Impact

  • Water Usage: Minimal due to closed-loop water systems
  • Emissions: Low emissions due to efficient processing
  • Energy Consumption: Optimized for energy efficiency
  • Waste Reduction: Diverts batteries from landfills
  • Material Recovery Rate: >95% recovery of critical materials

Company Information

Li-Cycle is a leading global lithium-ion battery resource recovery company and the largest lithium-ion battery recycler in North America. The company's innovative "Spoke & Hub" technologies allow for the recovery of critical materials from all types of lithium-ion batteries.

Processing Technology

Li-Cycle's Generation 3 technology uses a submerged shredding process that can safely and efficiently process full lithium-ion battery packs without discharging or dismantling them first. This innovative approach:

  • Improves safety by minimizing fire risk
  • Increases processing efficiency
  • Reduces manual handling requirements
  • Allows for processing of mixed battery chemistries
