Facility Overview

The Redwood Materials Nevada Campus is the company's flagship facility located in McCarran, Nevada. Founded by JB Straubel, former CTO of Tesla, Redwood Materials has established itself as a leading battery materials and recycling company in North America.

With a processing capacity exceeding 40,000 tonnes per year, the Nevada Campus is one of the largest lithium-ion battery recycling facilities in North America. The facility is designed to process various forms of battery materials, including manufacturing scrap, production waste, and end-of-life batteries from electric vehicles and consumer electronics.

What distinguishes Redwood Materials is its integrated approach - the facility not only recycles lithium-ion batteries but also manufactures new battery materials, creating a true closed-loop system. The Nevada Campus employs advanced hydrometallurgical processes to recover critical materials like lithium, nickel, cobalt, and copper at high purity levels, which are then used to produce battery materials for new batteries.

Processing Technology

The Nevada Campus utilizes an integrated hydrometallurgical process:

  • Initial mechanical processing and shredding
  • Advanced material separation techniques
  • Chemical leaching processes to extract valuable metals
  • Metal purification systems to achieve battery-grade purity
  • On-site production of anode copper foil and cathode active materials
  • Quality control systems to ensure consistent output

Regional Significance

The Nevada Campus is strategically located near Tesla's Gigafactory and other battery manufacturers, enabling efficient recycling of production scrap and minimizing transportation emissions. The facility has created hundreds of jobs in the region and contributes to establishing a domestic battery supply chain, reducing dependency on imported materials and supporting the growth of the North American EV industry.
