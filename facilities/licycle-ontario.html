Operating

Facility Overview

The Li-Cycle Ontario Spoke in Kingston was the company's first commercial-scale facility and has been operational since 2019. This facility represents Li-Cycle's technological foundation and serves as proof of concept for their Spoke & Hub business model.

As the first facility in Li-Cycle's network, the Ontario Spoke processes various forms of lithium-ion battery waste including manufacturing scrap and end-of-life batteries from various sources, including consumer electronics, energy storage systems, and electric vehicles.

The facility utilizes Li-Cycle's patented technology to process lithium-ion batteries of various chemistries, formats, and states of charge in a safe and environmentally friendly manner. The process produces black mass, which contains critical metals such as lithium, cobalt, and nickel, which can then be processed at the company's Hub facility.

Technical Specifications

  • Processing Capacity: 5,000 tonnes per year
  • Technology: Mechanical processing
  • Start of Operations: 2019
  • Feedstock: Manufacturing scrap and end-of-life batteries
  • Output: Black mass and other recyclable materials

Environmental Impact

  • GHG Reduction: Lower carbon footprint compared to mining
  • Water Usage: Limited water consumption
  • Waste Diversion: Prevents batteries from entering landfills
  • Resource Recovery: >95% recovery rate of battery materials
  • Land Use: Repurposed industrial facility

Company Information

Li-Cycle is a leading global lithium-ion battery resource recovery company and the largest lithium-ion battery recycler in North America. The company's innovative "Spoke & Hub" technologies allow for the recovery of critical materials from all types of lithium-ion batteries.

Processing Technology

The Ontario Spoke utilizes a mechanical processing technology to safely shred and separate lithium-ion batteries into three main components:

  • Black mass (containing valuable metals like lithium, nickel, and cobalt)
  • Copper and aluminum
  • Plastics

This process enables the recovery of valuable materials while avoiding thermal processing, which can result in loss of materials and higher emissions.
